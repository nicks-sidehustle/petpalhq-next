# Research Template — Product Guide

**How to use:** Copy this file to `research/<guide-slug>.md` for each new product guide. Fill out every section. The Status checklist at the bottom is the gate before moving to the writing phase. Empty sections = research not done.

**Voice rule:** This is a research notebook, not editorial copy. Write in plain notes, not finished prose. Direct quotes from sources go in `> blockquotes`. Editorial phrasing comes later.

**Moat rule:** Every claim that appears in the published guide must trace back to at least one cited source in this document. We synthesize expert consensus AND owner experience; we do not invent either.

**Scoring rule:** Every product recommended in a published guide gets a **PetPalHQ Score (TBD brand)** for its scenario. The score is computed from sub-scores per dimension, weighted per scenario. Every sub-score requires ≥2 sources backing it (or 1 Tier 1 source). The score is the moat in numerical form — proprietary, defensible, traceable to research.

## 2. Owner Angles & Why It Matters

Beyond clinical accuracy, the published guide must address what pet owners actually care about. List the emotional, relational, and lifestyle dimensions of this topic. **This is a research charter** — it tells you what to look for in sources.

For each angle: phrase it as the question or worry an owner would have, identify the persona(s) it most affects, and note where the published guide will address it.

- **Worry:** "Will the wrong [product] hurt my pet over time?"
  - **Persona:** Sophia (worried optimizer), Priya (evidence-driven)
  - **Why it matters:** Health/safety stakes, long-term consequences, latent damage
  - **Where addressed in published guide:** Top pick rationale, vet deference

- **Daily-life friction:** "Can I actually use this thing day-to-day without fighting my pet?"
  - **Persona:** Elena (lifestyle-driven), all owners with reactive pets
  - **Why it matters:** Friction reduces use; an unused product fails the pet
  - **Where addressed:** Trade-offs in candidates

- **Aspiration / bond:** "I want my pet to enjoy [this activity] more, not endure it"
  - **Persona:** All
  - **Why it matters:** Quality of relationship; pet-owner bond is the underlying motivation
  - **Where addressed:** Editorial intro

- **Dignity (especially seniors):** "My pet is aging — I want them comfortable, not constrained"
  - **Persona:** Sophia
  - **Why it matters:** End-of-life care, comfort over convenience
  - **Where addressed:** Joint-issues / senior scenario, vet deference

- **Cost calculation:** "Is the upgrade worth the money?"
  - **Persona:** Marcus (buy-once-cry-once), Elena (lifestyle)
  - **Why it matters:** Owners distinguish "saving money now" from "saving money over years"
  - **Where addressed:** How we picked, comparison table

Aim for **5–10 angles**. Cover at least one worry, one aspiration, one daily-friction angle, and one dignity/bond angle. These shape the published guide's tone and inform which scenarios to feature.

## 8. Scoring Rubric — Scenario-Specific

**The moat in numerical form.** Every candidate product gets a score per scenario, computed from sub-scores against weighted dimensions. The score is proprietary to PetPalHQ and is defensible because every sub-score traces to sources.

### Why scenario-specific?

A great harness for everyday walks isn't necessarily a great harness for road trips. The dimensions that matter shift by scenario, and the weights shift accordingly. A single "best harness" score is misleading; a scenario-conditional score is honest and matches the editorial framework (no price tiers — scenarios decide the recommendation).

### Step 1: Define dimensions for THIS guide

Dimensions are category-specific. For dog harnesses they might be Comfort, Ease of use, Safety, Durability, Fit precision. For dog beds they might be Comfort, Therapeutic support, Durability, Cleanability, Size/fit. Pick 4–6 dimensions that cover the meaningful evaluation axes.

### Step 3: Scoring scale (0–10 per dimension)

Apply the same scale across all dimensions. The scale is qualitative but disciplined.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 9–10 | Exceptional. Multiple high-tier sources confirm. Few or no caveats. Best-in-class on this dimension. |
| 7–8 | Strong. Sources converge favorably. Minor caveats acknowledged. |
| 5–6 | Mixed. Sources diverge OR evidence is thin OR fits some uses but not others. |
| 3–4 | Weak. Sources note significant concerns. Notable owner-voice complaints. |
| 0–2 | Disqualifying. Serious safety, quality, or ethical issues from credible sources. Excluded from candidates. |

### Step 4: Audit rules

- **Every sub-score requires ≥2 sources backing it** (or 1 Tier 1 source).
- **Sub-scores ≤4 must be explained in the published guide** (why we passed on or downscaled the product on this dimension).
- **Scoring is recorded in §9 (Candidate Products) per scenario.**
- **Weighted totals appear in §10 (Top Pick) and §11 (Scenario Alternatives).**

## Handoff to writing

When all boxes above are checked, the writing phase has these as inputs:
- §1 Topic Brief → article framing, audience targeting, headline
- §2 Owner Angles → editorial tone, scenario selection, intro phrasing
- §4 Stats → opening hooks, mid-article anchors, FAQ context
- §5 Owner Voice → pull quotes, FAQ texture, the published guide's tonal register
- §6 Convergence → editorial backbone, the moat narrative
- §7 Divergence → "the honest truth is..." framing
- §8 Scoring Rubric → "How we picked" methodology recap on the published page
- §9 Candidate scoring → the audit trail behind every score the reader sees
- §10 Top Pick → the signature top-pick card on the published page (with displayed PetPalHQ Score)
- §11 Scenarios → the three "Also Worth Considering" sections (each with displayed PetPalHQ Score for that scenario)
- §12 Passed On → optional "What we passed on" section in the published guide
- §13 FAQ → published guide's FAQ section (`<details>` items)
- §14 Vet deference → mandatory veterinary callouts in relevant sections
- §15 Schema → JSON-LD enrichment, including PetPalHQ Score structured data

The research file is preserved alongside the published guide. **It IS the audit trail — including for every score.**
