## Maintenance guidance

The key article angle for PetPalHQ should be that cleaning tools are not just about making glass look nicer. They lower maintenance friction, and that matters because skipped maintenance is one of the fastest paths to poor water quality. Partial water changes dilute dissolved waste, substrate cleaning removes trapped organics before they keep decomposing, and careful filter maintenance helps preserve flow and biofiltration instead of letting gunk choke it down. ## Reader questions and linking

**FAQ**

**Do aquarium cleaning tools really improve water quality, or do they just make the tank look better?**  
They do both. Glass scraping is the most cosmetic task on the list, but water changers, gravel vacs, siphons, and hose brushes directly affect water quality by exporting waste, lowering accumulated organics, and keeping filtration and maintenance practical enough that it actually happens on schedule. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn21view0Ņłéturn36search2Ņłéturn40search0Ņłü

**How often should I change water if the aquarium looks clean?**  
ŌĆ£Looks cleanŌĆØ is not a reliable metric, because dissolved waste is invisible. A good beginner default is a weekly 20ŌĆō30% change, then adjust based on stocking, feeding, and water-test results. Lightly stocked tanks sometimes go longer, but heavily stocked tanks can need more than weekly or larger changes. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn35search4Ņłéturn43search9Ņłéturn36search2Ņłü

**Can I use a gravel vacuum on sand?**  
Yes, but tool choice and technique matter. Fine sand is where gravel-only siphons and oversized vac heads are most likely to clog or remove too much substrate. For sand, you usually want lighter surface hovering or a gentler workflow rather than deep plunging. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn32search12Ņłéturn22view0Ņłéturn39view0Ņłü

**Are magnetic cleaners safe for acrylic?**  
Only if they are explicitly acrylic-safe and used carefully. Flipper includes an ABS plastic blade for acrylic, Aqueon includes acrylic pads with its magnet, and Mag-Float sells separate acrylic-specific versions. Using the wrong blade or trapping grit under any magnet is where scratch risk starts. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn22view0Ņłéturn23view0Ņłéturn16view0Ņłéturn34search22Ņłü

**Should I clean my filter every water change?**  
Not necessarily. Inspect it regularly and clean when flow is dropping or when mechanical media is visibly loaded, but avoid replacing or aggressively stripping all bio-relevant media at once. The goal is maintenance, not sterilization. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn36search1Ņłéturn36search5Ņłéturn43search9Ņłü

**Can I use dish soap to clean aquarium tools or the tank itself?**  
Not for anything that goes back into the aquarium. Aquarium Co-Op explicitly says not to use soap, and Aqueon reserves cleaner use for the outside of the glass and specifies aquarium-safe cleaner there. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn37search4Ņłéturn37search9Ņłü
