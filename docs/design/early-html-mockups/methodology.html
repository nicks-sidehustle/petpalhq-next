Methodology

How we research.

The seven commitments behind every PetPalHQ recommendation.

  1. 01

    We synthesize expert reviews. We don't test products ourselves.

    Placeholder body — the PetPalHQ team reads veterinary literature, certified trainer assessments, behaviorist publications, and professional reviewers (Wirecutter, Consumer Reports, The Spruce Pets), then identifies where professional opinion converges. That convergence is more reliable than any single tester's experience.

  2. 02

    Every guide cites 20+ professional sources.

    Placeholder body — we don't make recommendations on hunches or affiliate revenue. Each guide is built from a minimum of twenty independent expert sources, with veterinarian or specialist input on any health-related claim.

  3. 03

    We name our experts.

    Placeholder body — every clinical claim is attributed. If a study supports a recommendation, we name the institution and link to the source. If a vet flagged a concern, we say which vet — or which credential — and why.

  4. 04

    No paid sponsorships. No brand deals.

    Placeholder body — no company has paid us to feature their product. No product has been provided in exchange for placement. Our recommendations are independent of any commercial relationship beyond Amazon Associates affiliate links.

  5. 05

    Affiliate links are disclosed before the buy button.

    Placeholder body — we earn a small commission when readers buy through Amazon. That commission keeps the publication independent. It does not influence which products we recommend, and the disclosure appears on every page that contains an affiliate link.

  6. 06

    Health claims include veterinary deference.

    Placeholder body — for any claim involving health outcomes (joint disease, kidney function, dental health, behavior modification), we either cite a peer-reviewed source or include "consult your veterinarian" guidance within the same section.

  7. 07

    We update guides as evidence changes.

    Placeholder body — recommendations aren't static. When a new clinical trial publishes or a product is recalled, we revisit and update the affected guides. Every guide shows a "Last Updated" date so readers can judge currency.
