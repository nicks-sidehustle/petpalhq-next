# PetPalHQ Editorial Identity

## 1. Brand Positioning

**PetPalHQ is an exotic-pet gear publication that earns trust through expert synthesis.** We're not a personal-keeper blog ("I tested this in my home aquarium"), and we're not a generic listicle aggregator. We sit in the same register as Wirecutter and The Strategist — but for aquarium, reptile, and bird hobbyists who genuinely care whether a UVB bulb works at the right wavelength or whether a canister filter holds up over five years of use.

**The competitive moat:** synthesizing professional consensus from veterinarians, aquarists, herpetologists, and ornithologists is computationally expensive editorial work. AI-generated content can't easily reproduce it without the underlying source reading. That's the AEO/GEO defense against generic affiliate listicles flooding the SERP.

**Domain:** petpalhq.com (kept for SEO continuity from prior pet-content history)

**Display brand:** PetPalHQ

**Editorial register:** "We" plural — the editorial team, not a single first-person reviewer. The voice is *trusted advisor who actually read the research*, not a magazine columnist.

## 3. Voice Rules

### ALWAYS

- "We read 32 expert sources" — establish the synthesis depth up front
- "Across the reviews we surveyed, the consensus is..." — name the methodology
- "Here's the honest trade-off:" — pair every recommendation with its real downside
- "We'd recommend..." or "We landed on..." — first-person plural editorial team voice
- "Across multi-year owner reports, this consistently..." — durability framed as data
- Concrete numbers when source backs them: "32 expert sources," "10,900+ owner reviews," "5+ years of multi-tank use data"

### NEVER

- "I tested this in my tank/terrarium/yard" — no first-person reviewer persona
- "My family's dog/cat/iguana..." — no personal anecdotes
- "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "must-have," "you'll love it" — hype phrases
- "BUY NOW," "limited time," "lowest price ever" — affiliate-spam urgency
- "Comprehensive," "delve," "leverage," "elevate," "seamless," "cutting-edge" — AI-content tells
- ALL CAPS for emphasis (in body content)
- Manufacturer marketing claims as facts ("scientifically proven," "industry leading") — always source-attribute or rephrase

### Required structural moves per guide

- Open with what we read (source count, type) before product picks
- Every product pick paired with at least one **honest trade-off**
- Every guide includes a **What We Passed On** section naming products that didn't make the cut and why
- Every product gets a **PetPal Gear Score** with subscores visible (Expert Consensus 30% / Effectiveness 25% / Animal Safety 20% / Durability 15% / Value 10%)
- Affiliate disclosure paragraph before the first CTA

## 4. Content Pillars

| Pillar | Slug | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| Water Quality & Cycling | `water-quality` | Aquarium water testing, conditioners, bacteria starters, the nitrogen cycle |
| Aquarium Filtration | `aquarium-filtration` | Filters, filter media, ongoing maintenance |
| Aquarium Care & Cleaning | `aquarium-care` | Cleaning tools, water changers, algae management |
| Reptile Habitat | `reptile-habitat` | Enclosures, substrate, hides, humidity, habitat design by species |
| Reptile Lighting & Heat | `reptile-lighting` | UVB, basking lamps, heat mats, thermostats, lighting safety |
| Bird Feeders & Backyard | `bird-feeders` | Smart and standard feeders, bird baths, backyard birdwatching gear |
| Expert Care Guides | `expert-care` | Cross-vertical care guides — feeding, health, enrichment, species-specific advice |

**Boundary test:** every guide must answer "would a person who keeps fish, reptiles, or watches birds in their backyard genuinely find this useful?" If the answer requires a stretch (e.g., generic "best pet beds"), it doesn't belong here. Future verticals (small mammals, amphibians) will be added when research depth justifies it — they don't ride on the current research.

**The bird vertical for v2 launch is birdwatching/wild-bird gear** (smart feeders, suet, bird baths). The current research depth supports only this submarket. **However, the brand logo intentionally includes a parrot silhouette**, signaling that pet-bird (caged parrot, finch, cockatiel, budgie) coverage is a planned future direction. When pet-bird research is commissioned, treat it as a distinct vertical with its own pillars — different audience, different safety considerations (toxic chemicals, mirrored surfaces, foot health), different product categories. The two bird submarkets coexist under the same site umbrella but should not blend in any single guide.

## 5. Three-Tier Framework (when applicable to product roundups)

For product spokes, present picks in three explicit price tiers:

- **Best for the Money** (budget tier — under category-typical median)
- **The Sweet Spot** (recommended tier — "what we'd buy")
- **Worth the Splurge** (premium tier — for serious hobbyists or high-AOV needs)

Hub/authority pages don't need the three-tier framework — they're educational, not directly commercial.

For product spokes that don't fit a clean three-tier (e.g., "Best Test Kits" where the category is dominated by one product), label the picks honestly: "The default" + "When to step up" + "When to skip even that."

## 7. Cross-Link Strategy

PetPalHQ sits in a 5-site affiliate network. Cross-links should be editorial, not promotional.

| Sister site | When to link | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| **GardenGearHQ.com** | Outdoor backyard topics — bird feeders, garden ponds, outdoor reptile habitats | Reciprocal |
| **SmartHomeExplorer.com** | Smart pet feeders, smart aquarium controllers, smart cameras for pet monitoring | Reciprocal |
| **DeskGearHQ.com** | Office aquariums, workplace fish-tank setups | Outbound only (low overlap) |
| **ChristmasGearHQ.com** | Pet-themed holiday decor, pet stockings | Outbound, seasonal only |
| **FoodsInMovies.com** | No editorial overlap | Avoid |

**Boundary rule:** *Pet GEAR (tanks, lights, feeders)* → PetPalHQ owns. *Pet-themed home decor or gifts* → ChristmasGearHQ owns. Don't fight over scope; link cleanly.

**Density:** 1–2 contextual cross-links per guide, placed where editorially natural. Never a footer dump of "our network." The `/our-network` page handles the network directory.

## 9. Quality Gates

Before any guide ships:

1. **Source count visible** — "We read N expert sources" appears in the intro
2. **At least one honest trade-off** per recommended product
3. **What We Passed On** section present
4. **Banned phrases scan** — no "I tested," "game-changer," etc.
5. **Affiliate disclosure** before first CTA
6. **All ASINs verified** via `npm run validate:amazon` or manual browser check
7. **`npm run validate:content` passes** — schema validation
8. **`npm run audit:guides` shows correct counts**

AI-assisted drafting is allowed, but every guide must pass a human-readable scan for voice consistency before shipping. The "voice consistency test" is: a reader who knows the petpal voice can identify a draft as petpal vs Wirecutter vs generic-Amazon-affiliate within 30 seconds.

---

## 10. What's Out of Scope

- **Hands-on product testing claims** — the moat is synthesis, not testing. Don't claim experiences we didn't have.
- **Fictional reviewer personas** — Nick Miles is a real editor. Don't invent secondary "reviewer" personas to add fake authority.
- **Generic pet content** (e.g., "Best Dog Beds") — doesn't fit the exotic-pet vertical and dilutes topical authority.
- **Health diagnoses or veterinary advice** — informational only; always recommend consulting a vet for medical decisions.
- **Live animal sales** — gear only, no animal commerce.
