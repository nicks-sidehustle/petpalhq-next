## How to Build an Enrichment Rotation

Don't give your dog the same enrichment toy every day — they'll get bored. Rotate through 3-4 different types on a weekly schedule:

1. **Monday/Wednesday:** Puzzle feeder (Nina Ottosson or Trixie)
2. **Tuesday/Thursday:** Snuffle mat for meals (PAW5)
3. **Friday:** Frozen Kong (prep Thursday night)
4. **Weekend:** Lick mat during downtime

This rotation ensures your dog encounters different challenge types and doesn't habituate to any single toy.

## FAQ

### What if my dog gives up on puzzles?

Start easier. Fill every visible compartment so the dog immediately finds food. Gradually increase difficulty as confidence builds. Some dogs need 3-5 guided sessions before they understand the concept. Never punish a dog for not solving a puzzle — it defeats the purpose.

### Are puzzle feeders safe to leave with an unsupervised dog?

Snuffle mats and Kongs: yes, once your dog has demonstrated they don't shred/swallow fabric or rubber. Plastic puzzles (Nina Ottosson, Trixie): supervise until you're confident your dog doesn't chew the plastic components. LickiMats: supervise aggressive chewers who might bite through the silicone.

### How do I clean snuffle mats?

Machine wash cold, gentle cycle, no fabric softener. Air dry or tumble dry low. Wash every 1-2 uses — saliva and food residue build up quickly in the fabric strips.

### Can puppies use enrichment toys?

Yes — start at 8 weeks with Kong Puppy (softer rubber) and LickiMats. Introduce simple puzzles at 4-6 months. Avoid multi-step puzzles until 12+ months when problem-solving cognitive development is more advanced.
