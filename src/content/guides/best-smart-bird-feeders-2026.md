## Buying Considerations the Marketing Pages Do Not Highlight

**Camera angle and close-focus matter more than headline resolution.** Birdbuddy's published specs emphasize HDR/WDR, field of view, and 2.6-inch close focus alongside the 5 MP stills and 2K video. WIRED's 2026 testing repeatedly notes that capture reliability and field of view matter more than 4K marketing. The Birdfy 2's dual-lens system is a real upgrade on the close-focus axis specifically because birds visit at unpredictable distances.

**Mounting position drives almost everything.** Cornell Lab and the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service both advise placing feeders either within about three feet of reflective windows — close enough that birds cannot build up fatal collision momentum — or well past the danger zone, typically more than 30 feet away. National Audubon Society spacing guidance recommends pole systems with squirrel baffles, with meaningful distance from jump-off points like fences and tree limbs. None of those constraints are in the brand product photography.

**Subscription gotchas are the dominant total-cost-of-ownership variable.** Birdbuddy's support documentation says core feeder functions work without a membership; that is genuinely useful and is the reason the PRO ranks first in this guide. Birdfy ships multiple AI and cloud-service tiers, and WIRED's 2026 coverage flags wide variation across the category. The Birdfy Rookie's product listing makes the trade-off explicit: AI identification is by subscription. Read the current subscription terms on the brand's site, not just the marketplace listing, before assuming a budget price applies to the AI capability.

**AI identification accuracy caveats.** Birdbuddy's own bird-species-identification documentation says the model is machine-learning based and improves with more training data. Good Housekeeping's 2026 review observed misidentifications and "unrecognized" captures on fleeting visits. Treat AI species ID as a learning aid that complements Cornell Lab's Merlin app, not as a perfect field guide.

**Solar panel placement is the second-biggest setup variable.** Birdbuddy's solar roof guidance is explicit that panel orientation and direct sun matter for sustained charge. Yards with morning-only sun, deep tree cover, or north-facing exposure routinely need supplemental USB charging even on solar variants. That is rarely surfaced in flagship marketing.

**MicroSD card slots do not always work as local storage.** Birdfy's own support FAQ states that most of its products do not support microSD storage or playback for end users, even on models with a card slot. If long-term local control of footage matters to you, treat that as a hard buyer requirement and verify on the brand's spec sheet for the specific model — not on a marketplace listing.

**Q: Are smart bird feeders safe to use during avian influenza outbreaks?**
A: Authorities disagree, and the disagreement is regional. Cornell Lab of Ornithology, the National Audubon Society, and the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service publish detailed safer-feeding guidance — clean feeders on schedule, pause feeding when sick birds appear, store seed properly, place feeders away from collision hazards. Their position is that responsible feeding is compatible with healthy bird populations. The California Wildlife Health Lab and California Department of Fish and Wildlife take a more conservative position and recommend not feeding during outbreaks, especially near backyard poultry, ducks, pigeons, or captive birds. If your area is under an active avian influenza advisory, the more conservative position applies. The full discussion is in the [hub guide](/guides/smart-bird-feeders-backyard-birdwatching).
