whenNotToBuy: |
  Skip every kit on this page if you expect it to hold off determined predators unattended. Catio Spaces recommends 14-to-16-gauge welded wire for predator resistance and warns that the light mesh shipped with kits can be compromised if you can tear it by hand. A Home Depot reviewer said the AIR37's light boards would not stop a predator, and the Outback Jack offers no predator protection at all. In heavy coyote or large-dog territory, either reinforce a kit with heavier welded wire or commission a custom build.

  Skip the wooden kits if you cannot commit to maintenance. Catio Spaces advises sealing kit lumber annually and anchoring frames to a deck, house, or 4x4 ground frame to prevent tipping and wood rot. Soft fir that sits on bare wet ground degrades fast, and galvanized mesh can rust within months in damp climates per AIR52 owner reports. A catio is a structure, not an appliance.

  Skip the window-box format if your window geometry does not cooperate. The Coziwow design depends on careful alignment with your window height for safe entry; measure before ordering rather than improvising a ramp afterward.

  Skip the pop-up if anyone in the household will be tempted to leave the cat outside alone. Cats.com is explicit that the Outback Jack is not designed for unattended outdoor use — the zippers do not lock and the fabric mesh yields to sustained clawing.

  Finally, air out any enclosure that arrives smelling of chemical wood treatment before a cat goes in, and provide shade and water in every catio during summer heat. An enclosure a cat cannot escape is an enclosure you are responsible for cooling.

## What "Predator-Resistant" Actually Means

The single biggest gap between catio marketing and catio reality is predator resistance, and the experts are blunt about it. Catio Spaces, whose founder Cynthia Chomos builds custom catios professionally, recommends 14-to-16-gauge welded wire and offers a field test anyone can run: grab the mesh and pull — if you can tear it by hand, a predator can defeat it.

Shipped kits routinely fail that test. A Home Depot reviewer of the Aivituvin Walk-In put it plainly: the light 1x2 boards would not stop a predator. The AIR52 Cat Run uses galvanized wire that is more serious, but owners report razor-sharp tips where the wire meets the frame — file or cap every junction before a cat goes in — and rust appearing within months in damp climates. Catio Spaces adds a less obvious warning: vinyl-coated wire can crack as UV exposure ages the coating, so coated mesh is not a permanent upgrade either.

For owners in genuine coyote country, Jackson Galaxy's advice in the Consumer Reports guide is the design brief: build in vertical surfaces so cats can navigate the entire catio without touching the ground, and extend fencing underground so digging predators cannot tunnel in. Aerial threats are the easier problem — a solid roof cover, like the panels worth adding to the AIR37 or the cover shipped with the AIR52, blocks hawks while doubling as rain and shade protection. Anchor any freestanding kit to a deck, the house, or a 4x4 ground frame; an enclosure that tips in wind protects nobody. And two summer rules are non-negotiable for every format including the Outback Jack: shade and water, always. A cat who cannot leave an enclosure is a cat depending on you to keep it cool.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is it safe to leave a cat in a catio unsupervised?**

It depends entirely on the format. A wood-and-wire enclosure with latched doors — the Aivituvin AIR37, the Coziwow, or the AIR52 — can handle unsupervised daytime sessions in a typical suburban yard, with the caveat that light kit framing is not rated against large predators. The fabric pop-up is a different category: Cats.com states plainly that the Outback Jack is not designed for unattended outdoor use, because the zippers do not lock and the mesh yields to sustained clawing. Match supervision to your local wildlife, and when in doubt, stay outside with the cat.

**Will a catio protect my cat from coyotes and hawks?**

Hawks are the solvable half of that question — a solid roof cover blocks aerial attacks, which is one reason to add panels over the AIR37's mesh roof and a point in favor of the AIR52's included cover. Coyotes are harder. Jackson Galaxy, quoted in Consumer Reports' catio guide, advises vertical surfaces that let cats cross the enclosure without touching the ground, plus underground fencing extensions to stop diggers. No kit in this guide ships with either feature ready-made, so in coyote country a prefab catio is a starting platform that needs reinforcement, not a finished defense.

**What wire gauge should a catio use to resist predators?**

Catio Spaces recommends 14-to-16-gauge welded wire, and the hand-tear test is the practical check: mesh you can rip by hand is mesh a raccoon or coyote can breach. Most shipped kits use lighter material than that standard, which is why we score every pick here as containment rather than predator-proofing. If you upgrade, welded wire is the recommended spec, and be cautious with vinyl-coated products — Catio Spaces warns the coating can crack with UV exposure, leaving the wire beneath to corrode.

**Does a catio need a floor or buried wire to stop digging predators?**

If predators dig where you live, yes — the Consumer Reports experts recommend underground fencing extensions for exactly that reason. Among the picks, only the Outback Jack ships with a full floor: its mesh bottom blocks insects and pests from below, though the fabric is for supervised use only. For the wooden kits, anchoring solves most of the problem — Catio Spaces advises fixing the frame to a deck, the house, or a 4x4 ground frame, which closes the gap a digging animal would exploit and stops the structure from shifting or tipping in wind.

**How long do prefab wooden catio kits last outdoors?**

Plan in seasons, not decades, unless you maintain them. Catio Spaces recommends sealing kit lumber annually, and the soft fir in the Aivituvin kits — which Cats.com found splits easily under overdriven screws — needs that protection to survive wet winters. Metal is not exempt: AIR52 owners report galvanized mesh rusting within months in damp climates, with one panel described as close to rusting through, and replacement parts were out of stock when an owner asked. Keep wood sealed, keep frames off bare ground, file and recheck wire junctions yearly, and a kit catio can serve for years.
