For cats, calming starts with environment, not sedatives. The AAFP/ISFM Feline Environmental Needs Guidelines treat unmet environmental needs as the foundation of feline behavior problems — cats need a safe place, multiple separated key resources, predatory play, predictable interaction, and respect for their sense of smell. The 2024 AAFP Intercat Tension Guidelines add an essential refinement: subtle signs like staring, blocking, walking away, and hiding matter as much as overt fighting. "My cats don't fight" is not enough to conclude a multi-cat home is harmonious.

The picks below are editorial synthesis. ## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do cat pheromone diffusers really work?**
A: They may help some cats with some stress-related behaviors, especially as part of a broader environment-first plan. The Frank 2010 systematic review on feline pheromone evidence found that quality varies by use case. The Merck Veterinary Manual treats pheromones as one supportive layer inside a multi-modal plan rather than a standalone treatment. Pheromones are supportive tools, not guaranteed fixes. Pheromone response is individual. A non-response from your cat is not a sign of failure. It just means this is not the right adjunct, and the rest of the AAFP/ISFM five-pillar environment matters more.

**Q: Which is better — FELIWAY Classic, MultiCat, or Optimum?**
A: They are different formulations targeted at different use cases per Ceva's manufacturer documentation. Use FELIWAY Classic Spray for familiar-scent comfort and travel or targeted locations. Use FELIWAY MultiCat for multi-cat tension signs the AAFP 2024 Guidelines name. Use FELIWAY Optimum for broad household stress. They are not interchangeable, and stacking three of them in one room is not better than one in the right room.

**Q: How do I know if my cats have intercat tension?**
A: Look for the subtle signs the AAFP 2024 Intercat Tension Guidelines emphasize: staring, blocking, walking away, hiding, guarding routes (litter box, food station, doorway), and inhibited normal behavior. These matter alongside the overt signs like hissing, swatting, chasing, and fighting. The guidelines are explicit that subtle signs matter. "My cats don't fight" is not enough to conclude harmony. Pay attention to whether one cat consistently leaves the room when another enters. Check whether feeding and litter-box use have shifted to off-hours, or whether one cat has reduced their territory to a single room.

**Q: How many litter boxes do I need in a multi-cat home?**
A: A common rule is one per cat plus one extra, placed in different locations. The AAFP 2024 Intercat Tension Guidelines and the AAFP/ISFM Feline Environmental Needs Guidelines both emphasize the more important behavioral point: cats should not have to cross another cat's guarded territory to eliminate. Multiple boxes on the same floor of the same room is not "multiple boxes" in the behavioral sense. Distribute across rooms and floors so that any cat can reach a box without passing a tension point.

**Q: Can I spray pheromone directly on my cat?**
A: No. Spray bedding, carrier interiors, or target areas according to Ceva's manufacturer instructions, and let the area dry before the cat contacts it. FELIWAY Classic Spray uses an alcohol carrier. Spraying it directly on the cat is uncomfortable, defeats the purpose, and is not how the product is designed to work.
