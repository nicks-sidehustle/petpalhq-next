## Why Cresties Need Height, Humidity, and No Heat Lamp

Start with height, because it is the factor most buyers underweight. Crested geckos are arboreal — they live and hunt among branches — so they use vertical space far more than floor length, and a tall enclosure with cork, branches, and vines lets them climb and jump the way they naturally do. A common adult footprint is roughly 18 by 18 by 24 inches, which is why the Oiibo 18x18x24 sits at the standard and the 36-inch PROLEE 24x18x36 goes further for a display build. A short, wide tank wastes space a crestie will barely use, so when you compare a [vertical terrarium](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=18x18x24+vertical+reptile+terrarium&tag=petpalhq08-20), read the height first and favor lift over length.

Humidity and temperature are the other half. Cresties are tropical and need a humidity cycle in roughly the 60 to 80 percent range, achieved by daily misting, which is why a glass or polycarbonate enclosure that holds moisture matters — but it has to ventilate too, because stagnant, over-humid air causes respiratory infections. A screen top that lets air move while the walls hold humidity is the balance to look for. And here is the part that trips up new keepers: a crested gecko thrives at room temperature and generally needs no basking heat lamp, since sustained heat above the low 80s Fahrenheit is harmful. If a [starter kit](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=crested+gecko+glass+terrarium+screen+top&tag=petpalhq08-20) includes a halogen dome, as the Zilla does, you can usually set it aside and let your room temperature and misting do the work instead.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What size terrarium does a crested gecko need?**

A: A common adult minimum is roughly 18 by 18 by 24 inches of vertical space, which is why the Oiibo 18x18x24 sits at the standard and taller enclosures like the PROLEE 24x18x36 give even more climbing room. Height matters more than floor length because cresties are arboreal. A hatchling or juvenile can start smaller, in something like the 12x12x18 Clioran, and move up as it grows, but a full-grown adult should have at least that 18x18x24 footprint with branches and cover to climb.

**Q: Do crested geckos need a heat lamp?**

A: Usually not. Crested geckos thrive at ordinary room temperature, roughly the low-to-mid 70s Fahrenheit, and sustained heat above the low 80s is actually harmful to them. The halogen dome bundled with some kits, like the Zilla kit, is marketed broadly for tropical species, but a crestie generally does not need it. Unless your home is genuinely cold, rely on room temperature and daily misting rather than a basking lamp, and always check that your enclosure is not overheating.

**Q: How do I keep humidity right for a crested gecko?**

A: Aim for a humidity cycle in roughly the 60 to 80 percent range, created by misting the enclosure daily so it peaks after misting and dries down between sessions. A glass or polycarbonate enclosure with a screen top, like the picks here, holds moisture while still ventilating, which is the balance you want. Use a hygrometer to track it — the Clioran and Zilla kits include one — and make sure air moves through the screen top, because stagnant, over-humid air causes respiratory infections.

**Q: What is the difference between a crested gecko and a leopard gecko setup?**

A: They are nearly opposite. Crested geckos are arboreal and tropical, so they need a tall, humid, well-ventilated vertical enclosure at room temperature, misted daily. Leopard geckos are ground-dwelling desert animals that need a long, low tank with a warm basking zone, dry substrate, and a heat source. Do not reuse a leopard-gecko tank layout for a crestie — the horizontal shape, dry substrate, and hot lamp are all wrong for an arboreal, room-temperature climber, and forcing the mismatch causes chronic stress and illness.

**Q: Should I buy a kit or just the tank?**

A: It depends on your experience. A complete kit like the Clioran starter kit or the Zilla kit gives a beginner most of the gear and a setup guide in one box, which removes guesswork. A bare tank like the Oiibo, PROLEE, or REPTI ZOO gives an experienced keeper a better or larger enclosure to outfit with their own branches, plants, and misting gear. Kits win on convenience for first-timers; bare tanks win on quality and size for keepers who want to build the habitat themselves. Both are valid — just budget for the décor a bare tank still needs.
