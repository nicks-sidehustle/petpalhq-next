Pump noise is the number-one complaint owners have about cat water fountains, and the cause is rarely the pump being cheap — it is the water level dropping below the pump's minimum line. PetSafe's own Drinkwell noise-troubleshooting documentation explains the mechanism: when the reservoir falls too low, air gets drawn into the impeller and the pump runs partly dry, which is loud and shortens the pump's life. Pumps also get noisier over time as cat hair, debris, and mineral scale build on the impeller and shaft, adding friction. Cornell Feline Health Center and ISFM both recommend offering running water to encourage cats to drink, since cats evolved as desert animals with a low thirst drive — so the goal is not to avoid fountains, it is to choose one that stays quiet and keep it that way.

**Q: Why does my cat water fountain hum or get louder as the water level drops?**
A: Because the pump is running partly dry. PetSafe's support documentation explains that when the reservoir falls below the pump's minimum line, air gets pulled into the impeller, which is loud and shortens the pump's life. The fix is to refill before the level gets low. A larger reservoir, like the 2.8L Veken, buys more days of margin, and features like low-water alerts or anti-dry-burning protection are built to prevent this exact problem.

**Q: Are stainless steel or ceramic fountains quieter and safer than plastic ones?**
A: They are usually safer and often quieter. Preventive Vet links porous plastic to feline chin acne because bacteria hide in micro-scratches, and recommends stainless steel or ceramic, which resist biofilm and are easier to sanitize. On noise, ceramic and stainless deaden vibration better than thin plastic walls. BPA-free plastic is a self-certified marketing claim, not a safety standard, so it does not change this recommendation.

**Q: How often should I clean a cat water fountain and change the filter to keep it quiet?**
A: Replace the water daily, deep-clean the fountain and disassemble the pump at least weekly, and change filters every two to four weeks. PetSafe's documentation notes that hair, debris, and mineral scale on the impeller make any pump louder over time, so the weekly pump clean is what keeps a quiet fountain quiet. A clogged filter also restricts flow, which can make the pump strain and buzz.

If your cat is drinking far more or less than usual, straining, or showing lethargy, that is a veterinary question, not a fountain question.
